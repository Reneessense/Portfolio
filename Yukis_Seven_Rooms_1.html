Yuki's Seven Rooms

An immersive VR narrative experience

Abstract

Yuki's Seven Rooms is an immersive Virtual Reality narrative experience that utilizes volumetric capture and poetic interfaces to explore themes of memory, identity, and loss. Participants navigate seven distinct, non-linear architectural spaces, each containing fragments of the protagonist Yuki's life. The experience is designed to challenge the linearity of traditional storytelling, inviting users to actively reconstruct Yuki's identity through temporal and spatial perception.

VR Design & Interaction

The core mechanic involves "memory architecture," where the environment changes based on the user's focus. Interactions are minimal and symbolic, such as activating time-lapses of specific objects or listening to binaural audio snippets. We experimented with a low-fidelity, stylized aesthetic to emphasize the abstract nature of memory.

Research Focus

The project functions as a probe into how the design of virtual spaces affects user engagement with complex emotional narratives. We are particularly interested in the role of embodied presence and self-reflection within a fragmented, non-player-character-driven environment.
