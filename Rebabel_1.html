Rebabel

A Speculative Design Critique on Universal Translation

Abstract & Concept

"And the Lord said, Behold, the people is one, and they have all one language..."

Rebabel is a conceptual art and speculative design project that critiques contemporary communication technologies—specifically, translation engines and global communication platforms—by reimagining the biblical Tower of Babel.

By intertwining the theological narrative of division with current technological frameworks, Rebabel frames communication as both a spiritual and political act. It questions the idea of a 'universal' language offered by AI, highlighting the power structures and hidden biases embedded in these systems.

Visual Narrative

The visual composition borrows the aesthetics of illuminated manuscripts and speculative infographics, merging theological symbolism with data visualization to question the neutrality of “universal” technologies.

It uses a highly detailed, fragmented visual language to explore how meaning is lost, manipulated, or controlled when funneled through automated linguistic systems.
