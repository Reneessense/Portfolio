Touch Across Distance

Transforms light into a mirror of our digital entanglement.

Overview & Abstract

Touch Across Distance is an art installation that transforms light and reflection into a metaphor for our digital entanglement — hands pressed against mirrored acrylic panels, each gesture leaving a luminous residue of desire and surveillance.

Inspired by the aesthetics of screen interaction, the work visualizes how the act of touching a glowing surface embodies both intimacy and exposure. Layers of fluorescent acrylic and red-coated fingerprints echo the traces of everyday digital contact, turning the screen’s habitual gestures into a poetic study of fear, proximity, and gaze.

Concept & Design

Inspired by the aesthetics of surveillance and digital intimacy, the installation investigates how acts of clicking and touching on screens expose the body to the gaze of the machine.

The light structure is composed of mirrored and fluorescent acrylic panels, coated with red traces of fingerprints and smudges — visual residues of contemporary screen interaction. The hand silhouettes are crafted from black cardboard, forming a minimal yet expressive composition that balances material cost with luminous intensity.

Through embodied interaction, participants become both observers and observed, co-authoring a collective memory of fear and proximity — a poetic reflection on the anxiety of “touch across distance.”
