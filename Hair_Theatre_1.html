Hair Theatre: A Ritual of Bodily Revival

Exploring micro-emotions through tactile, sonic textile interaction

Abstract

Hair Theatre transforms the everyday frustration of tangled hair into a tactile and sonic expression of subtle, often unspoken "micro-emotions". These quiet moments of tension—too minor to explain yet strong enough to unsettle—are made tangible through a singular textile system: a felted structure of heat-pressed conductive PLA blended with real hair and wool. When touched or stretched, this material generates sound through capacitive sensing, allowing participants to feel, hear, and gently soothe the hidden emotions.

Through the fusion of material, body, and sound, the work transforms fragility into connection—revealing that even minor "breakdowns" can be moments of care and empathy. Intertwining handcraft and digital sensing, Hair Theatre translates embodied emotions into tangible form, exploring how subtle feelings can find clarity and resonance through material touch and sonic response.

Design & Mechanism

The core interaction mechanism is based on capacitive sensing, with sensors ingeniously integrated within a flexible textile structure felted from a blend of real hair, wool, and conductive PLA fibers.

At the heart of the installation are nine suspended felted clusters—each an entanglement of wool, human hair, and conductive PLA. These clusters are connected by conductive threads to form a unified capacitive sensing circuit. The conductive threads are deliberately tied with knots to enhance haptic friction, evoking the physical tension associated with tangled hair.

When a user touches or pulls a cluster, the capacitive sensing triggers a sonic response. Each cluster (node) triggers one of nine fixed pitches (A-G, plus higher octave A-B), which layer to create a polyphonic emotional field, translating emotional friction into sonic resonance.

Context & Authors

The work is exhibited as an immersive interactive installation, aiming to transform the suppressed, minor "breakdowns" of daily life into a silent ritual of bodily resonance and renewal. Through tactile and sonic interaction, the piece offers a way to dialogically engage the body, where care is enacted through vibration, touch, and resonance.

The work is authored by Xinyi Pu (xpux@pratt.edu) and Mengqi Zhao (mzhao31@pratt.edu), both from Pratt Institute.
