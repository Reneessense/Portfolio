ShoulDex

A Panel-Woven Textile Sensing System

Abstract

ShoulDex is an innovative panel-woven textile sensing system designed for Rotator Cuff (RC) rehabilitation training. It seamlessly integrates sensors into a wearable shoulder support fabric to monitor and evaluate the user's shoulder range of motion and movement quality during rehabilitation exercises in real-time. The system aims to provide a comfortable, non-invasive alternative to traditional expensive or bulky motion capture equipment, helping patients and physical therapists track progress remotely and ensure the accuracy of rehabilitation movements.

Design & Mechanism

We utilize advanced soft textile sensors and optical motion capture technology for data acquisition and processing to model shoulder movements. We created precise sensing channels and pressure/flexion sensing areas within the textile. This panel-woven structure not only ensures the fabric's flexibility and breathability but also the accuracy and consistency of the sensors across different shoulder postures.

The raw motion data collected by the textile sensors is analyzed using a **Multi-head LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) regression model** [cite: 828, 833] [cite_start]to accurately determine shoulder joint angles and classify compensatory movements[cite: 823]. We developed a microcontroller-based processing unit that resolves the textile sensing data and transmits it via Bluetooth to a mobile application for visualization and real-time feedback, enabling real-time motion monitoring.

Application & Impact

The primary application scenario for ShoulDex is home-based and remote rehabilitation. By using the textile sensors to identify joint movement angles and classify compensatory behaviors, the system provides a visual interface to help users correctly perform exercises even without a physical therapist present, thereby improving rehabilitation adherence and effectiveness. This design demonstrates the immense potential of smart textiles in the medical and healthcare domain.
